Working in Progress

The Political-Economy of China's Industrial and TFP Spatial Variance: A Dynamic Spatial General Equilibrium Approach

Co-author: Zhongji Wei

This research examines the political-economic mechanisms shaping the spatial distribution of China's industries and firms' total factor productivity (TFP). We develop a dynamic spatial general equilibrium model to study the interaction effects between heterogeneous enterprises and local governments, analyzing how government policies impact enterprise production. Through welfare analysis and counterfactual policy scenarios, we aim to identify key factors affecting TFP differences across Chinese industries and enterprises.

Completed Research Projects

The Impact of Third-Party Payment on The Profitability of Commercial banks in China

Graduation Thesis, Xi'an Jiaotong University

Advisor: Prof. Shen Gu

This thesis investigates how the rise of third-party payment systems has affected the profitability of commercial banks in China. Through empirical analysis using fixed effects and mediation models, the research explores the mechanisms by which third-party payments impact bank profitability, with specific focus on the mediating roles of liquidity and net interest margin.

The Impact of Executive Monetary Compensation on Corporate Performance: Evidence From The A-Share Market

Graduation Thesis, Xi'an Jiaotong University

Advisor: Prof. Xin Wu

This research examines the relationship between executive monetary compensation and corporate performance in China's A-share market. Using data from listed companies and employing fixed effects and mediation models, the study investigates how executive compensation influences corporate performance, particularly focusing on the mediating role of investment in enterprise innovation.

The Impact of Earnings per Share and Comprehensive Earnings per Share on Stock Prices: A Study Based on the China Real Estate Market

Research Project, Xi'an Jiaotong University

Advisor: Prof. Jiankui He

This project analyzes how earnings per share (EPS) and comprehensive EPS influence stock prices in China's real estate market. Through empirical research using fixed effects models, the study provides insights into the relative impact of traditional EPS versus comprehensive EPS metrics on stock performance in this specific sector.
